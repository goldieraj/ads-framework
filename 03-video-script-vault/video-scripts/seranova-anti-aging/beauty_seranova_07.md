## Key Beats

1. [0:00 - 0:14] The Problem & False Solutions - Shows the physical pain point (deep wrinkles) and systematically shoots down common, frustrating solutions (Botox, expensive creams). Psychological purpose: Agitate the problem and validate the viewer's frustration with past failures.
2. [0:15 - 0:22] The "Enemy" Mechanism - Uses 3D animation to explain why creams don't work (the skin barrier blocks them). Psychological purpose: Shifts the blame off the consumer for past failures and establishes scientific credibility.
3. [0:23 - 0:36] The Expensive Solution (Price Anchor) - Introduces Microneedling as the true solution, but immediately highlights the $600/session cost. Psychological purpose: Creates a high price anchor so the upcoming product feels like a massive bargain.
4. [0:37 - 0:56] The Product Reveal & "How It Works" - Introduces the Seranova at-home kit. Uses 3D animation to show needles bypassing the barrier to deliver peptides. Psychological purpose: Presents the accessible solution and builds trust through a logical, visual demonstration of the mechanism.
5. [0:57 - 1:36] Social Proof & Objection Handling - Rapid-fire Before/After photos mixed with text explaining how fast and painless it is (5 mins, every 2 weeks). Psychological purpose: Proves the product works on real women of the target demographic while overcoming objections about pain, time, and effort.
6. [1:37 - 1:45] Authority Stacking - Mentions 500,000 users, shows a TV morning show feature, and features a dermatologist holding the product. Psychological purpose: Eliminates skepticism through mass adoption, media validation, and medical endorsement.
7. [1:46 - 2:05] Risk Reversal & CTA - Highlights the 90-day money-back guarantee, tells the user exactly what to do (click the link), and presents a discount. Psychological purpose: Removes all financial risk, creates urgency, and drives the click.
