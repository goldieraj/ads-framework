## Early pattern conclusions

1. **RejuvaCare is using a small set of visual archetypes repeatedly**, then pairing them with many different long-form primary text angles.
2. The first visible archetypes are:
   - **Product-in-use at home**
   - **AI/cartoon pain illustration**
   - **Graphic surgery aftermath**
3. This supports the broader scaling hypothesis already seen in the copy:
   - Few visuals
   - Many copy/persona combinations
   - Heavy reuse of winners
4. The product-in-use shots are intentionally **unpolished and domestic**, which increases plausibility and reduces the feel of a polished ad.
5. The cartoon bed illustration is serving as a **clarity visual**, not a beauty visual. It explains the symptom immediately.
6. The scar image is a **fear visual** that likely pairs best with surgeon-regret / failed-surgery narratives.

## Key Insight: RejuvaCare's Visual Strategy

RejuvaCare runs ~720 image ads but uses **fewer than 10 unique visual concepts**. Their scale comes from:
1. Same image + different persona (Anna Thompson, Blane Schilling MD, Robert Brown, Brittany Collins, David Thompson, John Harris)
2. Same image + different copy angle (PT confession, 2AM wake-up, failed surgery, 3 doctors told me)
3. Same image + different geo/platform targeting (v6, v9, v10, SA variants)

This is the "few visuals, many copy variants" model at massive scale.
