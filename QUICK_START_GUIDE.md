# Hermes Ad Framework: Quick Start Guide

Welcome to the Hermes Ad Framework. This system is built on the analysis of over 25,000 top-performing Meta ads across the health, wellness, and gadget niches. It is designed to take you from a blank page to a scaled, highly profitable ad account.

This Quick Start Guide will walk you through the exact sequence of steps to deploy the framework.

---

## Phase 1: Preparation & Asset Creation

Before you launch any campaigns, you must build your core assets using the frameworks provided.

**Step 1: Define Your Core Mechanism & Angles**
* Read: `CORE_AD_FRAMEWORK.md`
* Action: Identify the "Villain" (what your customer is currently doing that doesn't work) and your "Mechanism" (why your product actually works). 
* Action: Select 3 distinct "Personas" to test (e.g., The Expert, The Frustrated Patient, The Observer).

**Step 2: Write Your Scripts & Copy**
* Read: `HOOK_LIBRARY.md` and `SCRIPT_TEMPLATES.md`
* Action: Draft 3 distinct Video Scripts using the 5-Part Direct Response Structure.
* Action: Draft 3 long-form Advertorial text posts (1,000+ words) using the Image Framework guidelines.

**Step 3: Produce the Creative (The AI Pipeline)**
* Read: `AI_PRODUCTION_PIPELINE.md` and `IMAGE_FRAMEWORK.md`
* Action (Video): Use ElevenLabs for voiceovers and HeyGen for AI avatars if you do not have UGC. Source high-quality B-roll.
* Action (Image): Source "Ugly Ads" — low-production, native-looking images, medical illustrations, or raw ingredient shots. **Do not use text overlays on your best image ads.**

---

## Phase 2: Launch & Testing

The testing phase is about finding the winning Hook and Persona.

**Step 1: The Hook Test**
* Take your best video concept and create 3-5 variations where ONLY the first 3 seconds (The Hook) are different.
* Take your best long-form advertorial copy and pair it with 3-5 different "Ugly Ad" images.

**Step 2: Campaign Setup**
* Launch an ABO (Ad Set Budget Optimization) or CBO (Campaign Budget Optimization) campaign.
* Broad targeting (let the creative do the targeting).
* Group your variations logically (e.g., Ad Set 1: Video Hooks; Ad Set 2: Image/Advertorials).

---

## Phase 3: Diagnosis & Scaling

Once your ads have run for 48-72 hours, you must diagnose their performance and decide what to do next.

**Step 1: Diagnose Performance**
* Read: `PERFORMANCE_DIAGNOSIS.md`
* Action: Check your metrics against the baseline KPIs:
    * **Hook Rate (3s Views / Impressions):** Target > 25%
    * **Hold Rate (ThruPlays / 3s Views):** Target > 30%
    * **Outbound CTR:** Target > 1.5%

**Step 2: Execute the Kill / Scale / Iterate Protocol**
* **KILL:** If Hook Rate is < 20% AND CTR is < 1.0% (The ad is dead).
* **ITERATE:** If Hook Rate is > 25% BUT CTR is < 1.0% (Great hook, weak CTA/Mechanism — rewrite the second half).
* **ITERATE:** If Hook Rate is < 20% BUT CTR is > 1.5% (Weak hook, great selling proposition — keep the body, test new hooks).
* **SCALE:** If Hook Rate > 25% AND CTR > 1.5% AND CPA is profitable (Increase budget by 20% every 24-48 hours).

---

## Phase 4: Maintenance & Retargeting

As you scale, you must protect your account from ad fatigue and capture the "warm" traffic that didn't buy on day one.

**Step 1: Deploy Retargeting**
* Read: `RETARGETING_STRATEGY.md`
* Action: Launch a dedicated retargeting campaign targeting 30-day website visitors and video viewers.
* Action: Use the 4 Pillars of Retargeting Creative (The "Us vs. Them" Chart, The Unboxing/Tactile Ad, The Founder's Guarantee, The "Objection Killer" FAQ).

**Step 2: Monitor for Ad Fatigue**
* Read: `AD_FATIGUE_DETECTION.md`
* Action: Watch for the "Death Cross" (Frequency rises above 2.5 + CPA rises by 20% + CTR drops by 20%).
* Action: When fatigue hits, execute a "Soft Refresh" (change the thumbnail/first 3 seconds) or a "Hard Refresh" (new persona/new angle entirely).
