## Pattern Analysis

**90%+ of top-performing link headlines are questions.** This is not a coincidence. Questions:
1. Create a curiosity gap (the answer is on the landing page)
2. Qualify the reader (if the question applies to them, they click)
3. Feel less "salesy" than statements

**The best question headlines contain a specific number or detail:**
- "525,585 minutes" (not "the rest of the year")
- "$400" (not "expensive")
- "0.003%" (not "barely any time")
- "$27K" (not "expensive surgery")

**Headlines that attack an enemy outperform neutral headlines:**
- "Why Pay $400 for Apps You'll Never Use?" (attacks Apple Watch)
- "Is Your Cheap Knee Brace Making Things Worse?" (attacks drugstore braces)
- "Is Your Prostate Pushing You Toward Surgery?" (attacks medical system)

## Template Formulas

1. **The Math Question:** "What happens to [body part] the other [specific number] [time units]?"
2. **The Enemy Question:** "Why pay $[price] for [enemy product] when all you need is [simple benefit]?"
3. **The Authority Quote:** "[Title]: '[Provocative statement about conventional wisdom]'"
4. **The Outcome Claim:** "[Specific action] My $[dollar amount] [Conventional Solution]"
5. **The Frustration Mirror:** "Tired of [specific annoying thing about current solution]?"
6. **The Curiosity Gap:** "What did [authority figure] say about [specific method/study]?"
