## Why This Is the Shortest and Most Efficient Version

At 115 seconds, this is nearly half the length of the Talking Shoulder (201s) and the Frustration Mirroring (185s) versions. It works because:

1. **Single villain, not three:** It only attacks stretching/PT. No cortisone teardown. No surgery teardown. This makes it faster and more focused.

2. **One analogy, deployed perfectly:** The door hinge analogy does all the heavy lifting. No houseplant, no tire patch, no starving tissue metaphor parade.

3. **Immediate mechanism:** The blood flow explanation takes 13 seconds total. No Japanese study. No 5-year clinical trial. Just "your shoulder is starving for blood."

4. **The "crying in the car" image:** This single detail ("hunched over in your car crying on the way home") is doing the work that other versions need 30 seconds of story to accomplish. It's so specific that anyone who's experienced it will feel personally called out.

### Best Use Case
This is the ideal **retargeting ad** or **Instagram Reels/Stories** version. It's short enough for placement in feeds where attention spans are lower, and the contrarian hook ("Stop stretching") is perfect for someone who's already seen a longer RejuvaCare ad and needs a different angle to push them over the edge.
