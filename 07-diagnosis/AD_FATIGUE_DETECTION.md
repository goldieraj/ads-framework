# Ad Fatigue Detection & Refresh Protocol

This document outlines how to identify when a winning ad is burning out and the systematic process for refreshing it without losing momentum.

## 1. Defining Ad Fatigue

Ad fatigue occurs when the target audience has seen an ad too many times, causing a decline in performance. It is *not* just a single bad day of performance; it is a sustained downward trend.

### The Warning Signs (The Fatigue Funnel)
Fatigue typically manifests in this specific order:
1.  **Frequency Increases:** The average number of times a user sees the ad climbs (e.g., moves from 1.5 to 3.0+).
2.  **CTR Declines:** Users stop clicking because they've seen it before.
3.  **CPC Increases:** Meta charges more because the ad is less engaging.
4.  **CPA Increases (The Final Symptom):** The cost to acquire a customer exceeds the target threshold.

*Note: A high frequency with a stable CPA is NOT fatigue. Do not kill an ad just because frequency is high if it is still profitable.*

## 2. The 3-Stage Fatigue Diagnosis

Before killing a fatigued ad, determine *what* part of the ad is fatigued.

*   **Stage 1: Hook Fatigue (Most Common)**
    *   *Symptom:* Thumbstop ratio drops significantly, but Hold Rate remains stable.
    *   *Meaning:* People are tired of the first 3 seconds, but the core message still works.
*   **Stage 2: Visual Fatigue**
    *   *Symptom:* CTR drops, CPA rises, but the angle hasn't been saturated in the market.
    *   *Meaning:* They are "banner blind" to the specific colors, faces, or layout of the ad.
*   **Stage 3: Angle/Offer Fatigue (Rarest)**
    *   *Symptom:* All metrics degrade, and multiple visual/hook variations fail to revive it.
    *   *Meaning:* The market is tired of the core promise or the offer is no longer competitive.

## 3. The Refresh Protocol (How to Revive a Winner)

Do not start from scratch when an ad fatigues. Use the "Spin-off" method to iterate on the proven winner.

### Level 1 Refresh: The "Hook Swap" (For Stage 1 Fatigue)
*   **Action:** Keep the exact same body copy, voiceover, and core B-roll. Change *only* the first 3-5 seconds.
*   **Execution:**
    *   Test a new visual hook (e.g., change the background, use a different prop).
    *   Test a new text overlay (e.g., change "Fix your back" to "Why your back hurts").
    *   Test a new audio hook (e.g., start with a question instead of a statement).

### Level 2 Refresh: The "Visual Overhaul" (For Stage 2 Fatigue)
*   **Action:** Keep the same script and angle, but change the visual delivery entirely.
*   **Execution:**
    *   Swap the Avatar (e.g., change from a "Clinical Authority" doctor to an "Empathetic Confessor" user).
    *   Change the format (e.g., turn a talking-head video into a text-on-screen montage).
    *   Update the B-roll to feel fresh and modern.

### Level 3 Refresh: The "Angle Pivot" (For Stage 3 Fatigue)
*   **Action:** The core message is dead. You must find a new way to sell the same product.
*   **Execution:**
    *   Return to the `ANGLE_GENERATION.md` matrix.
    *   If you were selling on "Pain Relief" (Desire Axis), pivot to "Time Saving" or "Status."
    *   Introduce a new "Villain" (e.g., shift from blaming "aging" to blaming "toxic ingredients").
