## The Core Architecture

This library runs **one product, one presale funnel, five angles** — all tested simultaneously across image and short video formats. The persona is a credentialed tester, not a brand voice. Every ad routes to the same ranking article.

The **single mechanism:** cordless electric motor (400 RPM) eliminates scrubbing. All angles circle back to this one insight.

---

## Angle 1: The Tester (Authority / Proof)

**Hook:**
> I tested 14 grill brushes over 8 weeks 🔥
> Stainless. Brass. Coil. Mesh. Electric. Most failed within a week.
> The winner wasn't even on most lists.
> Full 2026 ranking 👇

**Link Headline variants:**
- "We Tested 14 Brushes Over 8 Weeks"
- "The Honest 2026 Grill Brush Ranking"

**Link Description:**
> We tested 14 grill brushes over 8 weeks. Most failed the bristle shed test in under a week. Here are the 6 worth knowing about, ranked.

**Annotation:** The "I tested X things" hook is the workhorse of this library — it appears in ~40% of all ads. The specificity (14 brushes, 8 weeks) does the credibility work. The "winner wasn't even on most lists" creates a curiosity gap that forces the click. The fragmented list format (Stainless. Brass. Coil. Mesh.) creates fast scroll-stopping rhythm.

## Angle 3: The Skeptic Converted (Objection-as-Hook)

**Hook:**
> I bought this to prove it doesn't work 😂
> 90 seconds later, my grates were the cleanest they've been since I bought them.
> A whole season of buildup. Gone.

**Link Headline:** "I bought this to prove it doesn't work"

**Link Description:**
> A 22-year pitmaster tested 14 brushes expecting the electric one to fail. It didn't. Here's the full 2026 ranking.

**Annotation:** Objection-as-hook is one of the highest-converting structures in the vault. The reader's skepticism is mirrored back at them ("I thought it was BS too"), then immediately demolished with a specific result (90 seconds, full season of buildup). The emoji signals self-awareness and disarms defensiveness.

**Link Description:**

## Image Ad Copy Patterns (Static)

The image ads use **bold text-on-image** with comparison charts and product lineup shots. Key headline patterns observed:

- "AFTER 6 WEEKS, ONLY ONE BRUSH STAYED IN ROTATION."
- "COMPARING THE TOP 6 GRILL BRUSHES OF 2026"
- "THE BRUSH I ALMOST LEFT OUT WON. HERE'S THE FULL RANKING."

**Annotation:** The image ads function as visual proof of the "tester" persona. The comparison chart format (product logos side by side) borrows editorial credibility from consumer review sites like Wirecutter. The all-caps bold text is designed to read as a headline, not an ad.
