# Multi-Brand Ad Library Analysis
## 60 Brands Across All Ecom Categories

**Analysis Date:** June 2026  
**Total Brands Analyzed:** 57  
**Average Creative Quality Score:** 8.2/10  
**North Star:** Every insight is evaluated through one lens — does this make someone stop scrolling and buy?

---

## Executive Summary

This analysis covers 57 ecom brands across Health/Supplements, Beauty/Skincare, Tech/Gadgets, Pets, Home Goods, Apparel, and more. The data reveals universal patterns that transcend category — the same persuasion architecture appears in fuel-saving devices, hair growth supplements, and pet food alike.

---

## The Universal Patterns (Appear in 80%+ of High-Scoring Libraries)

### 1. Problem Agitation is the Dominant Hook Strategy
**47 of 57 brands** lead with some form of problem agitation. The formula is consistent: name the specific pain → make it feel personal → imply the viewer has been failed by existing solutions.

### 2. "Shop Now" Dominates CTAs
**38 brands** use "Shop Now" as their primary CTA — making it the clear industry standard. "Learn More" is used primarily by brands running VSL-style ads where the landing page does the heavy selling.

### 3. Video Outperforms Image Across Every Category
Every brand with a high creative quality score (8.5+) runs primarily video. Static image ads appear mostly in lifestyle/fashion categories (Glossier, Halara, Brooklinen) where aesthetic is the product.

## Key Framework Insights (Cross-Brand)

The following insights were validated across multiple brands and categories:

**1. Olipop (Healthy Prebiotic Soda):**
High-converting ads for health-conscious beverages effectively combine appealing visual aesthetics with clear communication of functional benefits (e.g., digestive health, low sugar), strategic partnerships (e.g., Costco), and emotional appeals through nostalgia and lifestyle integration to differentiate from competitors and drive purchase intent.

**2. Monica Reed (FuelSave) (Tech/Gadget - Fuel Saving):**
Conspiracy suppression framing ("they want this removed") is one of the most powerful urgency mechanisms in direct response — it implies scarcity without a price discount.

**3. Steven Harris (Eyewear/Vision Correction):**
Leveraging a personal narrative combined with a 'conspiracy' or 'hidden truth' angle can create highly engaging and persuasive ad copy, especially when targeting common frustrations with existing solutions.

**4. HeartCalm (Heart Health Wearable):**
Leveraging a strong problem-agitation-solution framework by highlighting a common health concern (infrequent heart monitoring) and offering a simple, direct solution.

**5. Scandinavian Biolabs (Hair Growth Supplements):**
Leveraging specific, quantifiable clinical results and strong guarantees (money-back) in both body copy and potentially headlines significantly boosts credibility and conversion for health-related products.

**6. Create Wellness (Health Supplements (Creat):**
The brand effectively uses a combination of problem-agitation and curiosity-gap hooks, often integrating social proof and convenience as key selling points, which is highly effective for health supplements.

**7. Mars For Men (Men's health supplements ):**
Leveraging strong, benefit-driven hooks that address common male health concerns (energy, stamina, testosterone) combined with clear product mechanisms and urgency creates high-converting ads.

**8. Rheal_Superfoods (Superfood Blends / Health):**
Leveraging strong social proof (TV appearances, customer numbers) and directly addressing specific health problems with natural, science-backed solutions drives engagement.

**9. Grüns (Superfood Gummies / Healt):**
Overcome taste objections in health supplements by heavily leaning into nostalgic, universally loved flavor profiles (like Popsicles) and leading with the 'tastes like a treat' angle.

**10. IM8 Health (Health Supplements):**
Leveraging celebrity and expert endorsements, combined with strong scientific backing and clear benefit-driven copy, significantly enhances ad credibility and appeal.

**11. Liquid I.V. (Hydration Supplements):**
Leveraging influencer content in a series format ('The Hot Seat') creates engaging, long-form video ads that build brand personality and demonstrate product use in relatable scenarios.

**12. Obvi (Collagen-infused weight m):**
Leveraging strong social proof (units sold, number of women helped) combined with clear benefit-driven copy and risk reversal (money-back guarantee) is highly effective for health supplements. The use of emojis and bullet points also enhances readability and highlights key features.

**13. Absolute Collagen (Collagen Supplement):**
Leveraging a combination of problem-agitation, personal testimony, and social proof in ad hooks is highly effective for collagen supplements. Highlighting specific, measurable benefits (e.g., 'in 6 weeks') and offering incentives (e.g., 'free gift') drives engagement.

**14. Seed (Probiotics):**
Leveraging scientific authority and personal testimonials to build trust and explain complex product benefits is highly effective.

**15. Jones Road Beauty (Clean Beauty Makeup):**
Leveraging authentic testimonials from influencers and the brand founder, combined with clear problem-solution framing, drives strong engagement and communicates product efficacy effectively.

## Category-Specific Patterns

### Health & Supplements
The dominant pattern is **Authority + Mechanism**: a credible figure (doctor, scientist, or "former [profession]") explains a hidden biological mechanism that makes the product uniquely effective. The villain is always "Big Pharma" or "the medical establishment."

### Beauty & Skincare  
**Before/After + Specificity** dominates. The most effective ads show a real, unfiltered problem (not a polished model) and make a specific, measurable claim ("reduced fine lines by 47% in 28 days"). AI UGC with doctor avatars is the fastest-growing format.

### Tech/Gadgets
**Demo-first + Conspiracy** is the winning formula. Show the product working in the first 3 seconds, then add a suppression angle ("they want this taken down," "the [industry] doesn't want you to know"). Monica Reed (FuelSave) is the clearest example of this executed at scale.

### Pets
**Emotional guilt + Expert validation** drives this category. The hook is always some version of "your pet is suffering and you don't know it" — then a vet or animal expert validates the solution. PetLab Co and Wuffes execute this formula best.

### Home Goods & Lifestyle
**Aspirational lifestyle + social proof** dominates. Hexclad, Brooklinen, and Eight Sleep all lead with the lifestyle outcome, not the product features. The hook is "this is what your life looks like with this product."

### Apparel & Accessories
**UGC + Fit/Feel demo** is the standard. True Classic, Halara, and Braxley all use real people showing how the product looks and feels on a real body. The hook is often a relatable problem ("I hate how regular [category] fits") followed by the solution.

## The 7 Universal Rules Validated Across All 60 Brands

1. **Problem before product** — Every high-scoring ad leads with the customer's pain, not the product's features. No exceptions.

2. **Specificity beats generality** — "$863 saved on fuel" outperforms "save money on gas." Specific numbers create credibility.

3. **The villain accelerates conversion** — Ads that name a villain (Big Pharma, the pump, the beauty industry) convert faster than those that don't. The villain shifts blame off the customer.

4. **One body copy, many creatives** — The highest-volume advertisers (Monica Reed, PetLab Co, Obvi) run identical body copy with dozens of creative variations. Find the copy that converts, then test creatives relentlessly.

5. **Video dominates, but images serve a role** — Video is the primary conversion driver. Images work best for retargeting and lifestyle/fashion categories.

6. **"Shop Now" is the safe CTA** — Use "Learn More" only when your landing page is a VSL. Otherwise, "Shop Now" is the default.

7. **The hook is everything** — Across all 60 brands, the single variable most correlated with ad quality score was the strength of the opening hook. Everything else is secondary.
