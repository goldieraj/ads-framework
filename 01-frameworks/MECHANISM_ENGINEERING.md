# Hermes Mechanism Engineering System

## What is a Unique Mechanism?
A Unique Mechanism is the logical explanation for *why* your product works when everything else the customer has tried has failed. It gives the logical brain permission to buy what the emotional brain already desires.

If your product does not have a patented, scientific mechanism, Hermes must invent a conceptual framing for one.

---

## The 4 Types of Mechanisms

Hermes categorizes mechanisms into four archetypes. Choose the one that best fits the product and the target persona.

### 1. The Actual Mechanism (The Patent)
Use this when the product has a genuinely unique, proprietary, or patented component.
- **Formula:** `[Proprietary Ingredient/Tech] + [Specific Biological/Physical Action] = [Result]`
- **Example:** "The micro-encapsulated hyaluronic acid penetrates 3 layers deep instead of sitting on the surface."
- **Best For:** Skincare, tech gadgets, supplements with proprietary blends.

### 2. The Unspoken Mechanism (The Secret Science)
Use this when the product uses a common ingredient or technology, but the competitors aren't talking about *how* it works. You claim the mechanism by being the first to explain it.
- **Formula:** `[Common Ingredient] + [Detailed Explanation of How It Works] = [Result]`
- **Example:** "Most people don't know that Magnesium Glycinate crosses the blood-brain barrier to bind with GABA receptors, physically forcing your brain to power down."
- **Best For:** Commodity supplements, basic fitness gear, standard home goods.

### 3. The Conceptual Mechanism (The Metaphor)
Use this when the actual science is too boring or too complex. You invent a metaphor that makes the mechanism instantly understandable.
- **Formula:** `[Familiar Concept] + [Application to Problem] = [Result]`
- **Example:** "The Starvation Mechanism: Your joints aren't broken, they are starving. This supplement feeds them."
- **Best For:** Pain relief, weight loss, complex health issues.

### 4. The Anti-Mechanism (The Subtraction)
Use this when the product's value comes from what it *doesn't* have.
- **Formula:** `[Competitor's Harmful Ingredient] - [Your Product] = [Result]`
- **Example:** "The reason you're bloated is the hidden seed oils in your 'healthy' greens powder. We removed them."
- **Best For:** Clean eating, natural beauty, minimalist tech.

## How to Engineer a Mechanism (The Hermes Process)

When given a product without a clear mechanism, Hermes will run this process:

1. **List the Ingredients/Features:** What is actually in the product?
2. **Find the Action:** What does that ingredient/feature physically do?
3. **Translate to Metaphor:** How can that action be explained to a 5th grader?
4. **Name It:** Give the mechanism a proprietary-sounding name (e.g., "Cellular Starvation," "The Deep-Sleep Axis," "Micro-Penetration Technology").

## The "So What?" Test
Every mechanism must pass the "So What?" test. If you state the mechanism, and the customer can say "So what?", it is not finished. You must connect the mechanism directly to the desired outcome.
