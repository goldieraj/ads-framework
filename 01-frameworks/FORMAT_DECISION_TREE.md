# Hermes Ad Format Decision Tree

## Selecting the Right Vehicle
An angle is the message; the format is the vehicle that delivers it. Choosing the wrong format for a product will kill the conversion rate, even if the copy is perfect.

Hermes uses this decision tree to determine the optimal visual format for a given product and angle.

---

## 1. Is the product highly visual? (Does it DO something you can see?)
*Examples: A cleaning spray wiping away rust, a makeup foundation covering acne, a gadget cutting a vegetable.*

**IF YES → Use The Live Demo Format**
- **Structure:** Open immediately with the product in action. Do not show a face for the first 3 seconds. Let the visual transformation be the hook.
- **Why it works:** It provides instant proof. The brain processes visual transformation faster than spoken words.
- **Execution:** Split-screen (Before/After), extreme close-up, or hyper-lapsed demonstration.

**IF NO → Proceed to Question 2.**

---

## 2. Does the product require complex scientific explanation?
*Examples: A gut-health supplement, a red-light therapy device, a nootropic brain booster.*

**IF YES → Use The Authority Persona Format**
- **Structure:** Open with a bold claim or warning, then cut to a Doctor/Scientist/Expert in a clinical setting to explain the Mechanism.
- **Why it works:** Complex claims require high trust. A UGC creator explaining cellular ATP production triggers skepticism. A doctor explaining it triggers compliance.
- **Execution:** White coat, clinical background, 3D medical animation B-roll overlaid during the explanation.

**IF NO → Proceed to Question 3.**

---

## 3. Is the primary pain point deeply emotional or embarrassing?
*Examples: Severe hair loss, chronic bad breath, extreme debt, marital issues.*

**IF YES → Use The UGC Confessional Format**
- **Structure:** Shot on a front-facing phone camera, usually in a car or bathroom. Intimate, low-production value. The creator whispers or speaks vulnerably about their struggle.
- **Why it works:** It disarms the viewer's "ad radar." It feels like a friend sharing a secret, which lowers the skepticism barrier for highly sensitive topics.
- **Execution:** No flashy text overlays in the first 5 seconds. Raw audio. High emotional resonance in the Agitation beat.

**IF NO → Proceed to Question 4.**

---

## 4. Is the product a status symbol or lifestyle upgrade?
*Examples: High-end athleisure, luxury watches, aesthetic home decor.*

**IF YES → Use The Aesthetic Mashup Format**
- **Structure:** Fast-paced cuts of attractive people using the product in aspirational environments, driven by a trending audio track.
- **Why it works:** The buyer is purchasing an identity, not a solution to a problem. The ad must visually communicate that identity.
- **Execution:** Heavy focus on lighting, styling, and music sync. Less focus on verbal explanation.

---

## The Default Fallback: The Frankenstein Ad
If the product does not strongly fit any of the above, or if the user has zero budget for custom shooting, Hermes defaults to the Frankenstein Format.

- **Structure:** A voiceover (human or high-quality AI) reading the 5-Beat script, layered over relevant stock footage, static product images, and aggressive text overlays.
- **Why it works:** It relies entirely on the strength of the copy and the pacing of the edit. It is infinitely scalable and testable without a camera.
