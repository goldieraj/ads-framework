# Hermes Creative Testing Strategy

## The Reality of Meta Ads

No one, not even Hermes, knows with 100% certainty which ad will win before it runs. The goal of the creative strategist is not to predict the winner perfectly; it is to engineer a testing matrix where the winner reveals itself quickly and cheaply.

## The 3-Phase Testing Protocol

Hermes advises users to run creative through this specific protocol to find scaleable winners.

### Phase 1: The Angle Test (Finding the Message)
- **Goal:** Determine which psychological premise (angle) resonates with the market.
- **Method:** Take 1 Product. Generate 3 to 5 completely different Angles (using the 6-Axis Matrix).
- **Execution:** Produce 1 baseline video or image for each angle. The visual format should be simple (e.g., UGC talking head). Keep the variables low.
- **Metric to Watch:** CTR (Click-Through Rate) and CPA (Cost Per Acquisition).
- **Outcome:** Kill the losers. Identify the 1 or 2 winning angles.

### Phase 2: The Hook Test (Finding the Attention)
- **Goal:** Maximize the attention-capture of the winning angle.
- **Method:** Take the 1 winning Angle from Phase 1. Generate 5 to 10 different Hooks (the first 3 seconds) for that exact same body copy.
- **Execution:** Produce 5-10 variations of the video, where only the first 3-5 seconds change. The rest of the video remains identical.
- **Metric to Watch:** Hook Rate (3-second video plays / Impressions) and Hold Rate.
- **Outcome:** Identify the 1 or 2 hooks that stop the scroll at the highest rate.

### Phase 3: The Format/Visual Test (Finding the Scale)
- **Goal:** Take the winning Hook + Angle combination and find the visual format that scales the furthest before fatiguing.
- **Method:** Take the winning script. Produce it in 3 to 4 different visual formats.
- **Execution:** 
  - Format A: UGC Testimonial
  - Format B: Doctor/Authority Persona
  - Format C: Text-on-Screen / B-Roll montage
  - Format D: Split-screen demonstration
- **Metric to Watch:** ROAS (Return on Ad Spend) at high spend volume.
- **Outcome:** The ultimate winning creative that can be scaled horizontally.

---

## The "Frankenstein" Iteration Loop

Once an ad is scaling (Phase 3 winner), it will eventually fatigue. Hermes prevents fatigue using the Frankenstein method:

When CPA starts rising on a winning ad, Hermes will instruct the user to:
1. Keep the exact same audio track.
2. Swap out the B-roll in the middle of the ad.
3. Change the text overlay colors or fonts.
4. Add a new 1-second pattern interrupt at the beginning.

This creates a "net new" ad in the eyes of the Meta algorithm, resetting fatigue without requiring a new shoot or expensive shoot.
