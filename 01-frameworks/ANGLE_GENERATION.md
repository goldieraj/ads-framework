# Hermes Angle Generation System

## The Philosophy of Angles

An "angle" is not a hook. A hook is the first 3 seconds of an ad. An angle is the underlying psychological premise that makes the viewer care. 

To scale a product on Meta, you cannot rely on one angle. You must test multiple, distinct psychological entry points to find which one resonates cheapest and scales furthest.

Hermes uses the **6-Axis Angle Matrix** to generate distinct campaigns for the same product.

---

## The 6-Axis Angle Matrix

When generating creative for a product, Hermes will build one script/concept for each of these six axes.

### 1. The Enemy Axis (Us vs. Them)
- **Premise:** The viewer's problem is not their fault; it is the fault of a specific enemy who has been lying to them.
- **Structure:** Expose the lie -> Reveal the hidden truth -> Present the product as the rebellion.
- **Example:** "Why the $40 billion skincare industry is terrified of this $30 tallow cream."

### 2. The Mechanism Axis (The Science Lesson)
- **Premise:** The viewer's problem persists because they've been treating the symptom, not the root cause.
- **Structure:** Explain the true root cause using a simple analogy -> Introduce the unique mechanism -> Show the proof.
- **Example:** "Your shoulder isn't broken, it's starving. Here's how to feed it."

### 3. The Identity Axis (The Aspirational Shift)
- **Premise:** Using this product signals that the viewer belongs to an elite, intelligent, or highly capable group.
- **Structure:** Highlight a specific type of person -> Show the secret tool they use -> Invite the viewer to join them.
- **Example:** "The 12-minute morning ritual used by 40+ athletes to stay injury-free."

### 4. The Regret Axis (The Cost of Inaction)
- **Premise:** The pain of staying the same is far worse than the cost of buying the product.
- **Structure:** Agitate the long-term consequence of the problem -> Present the immediate, simple fix -> Create urgency.
- **Example:** "Don't wait until you're facing a $38,000 surgery. Fix the joint starvation now."

### 5. The Epiphany Axis (The "Aha" Moment)
- **Premise:** The viewer has been doing something "normal" that is actually causing their problem.
- **Structure:** Call out the common mistake -> Explain why it's harmful -> Provide the counter-intuitive solution.
- **Example:** "Stop putting ice on your joints. You're actually slowing down the healing process."

### 6. The Proof Axis (The Undeniable Demonstration)
- **Premise:** The product's effectiveness is so visually obvious it doesn't need a complex argument.
- **Structure:** Extreme visual demonstration -> Simple explanation -> CTA.
- **Example:** The "Wind Test" showing the EpiCooler blowing papers across a room.

---

## How Hermes Executes the Matrix

1. Receive the completed Product Brief.
2. Select 3 to 6 axes from the matrix based on the target persona.
3. Generate a complete 5-Beat Video Script or Single-Frame Image Concept for each selected axis.
4. Present the matrix to the user for testing.
